Debuff Immunity reworked
Debuff Immunity has been reworked into Magic Immunity, to be more understandable and systematic, both function-wise and naming-wise.
 
Magic Immunity is made up of two major components:
 
Magic Resistance. Works the same way as before, decreases the magic damage dealt to you by a percentage. When this percentage is 100%, we can also refer to it as Magic Damage Immunity.
 
Debuff Resistance. This one simply reduces the duration of debuffs by a percentage. When this percentage is 100%, we can also refer to it as Debuff Immunity.
 
Debuff Immunity and Magic Damage Immunity, when combined is what we call Magic Immunity. An infamous item that grants this state is none other than Black King Bar.
 
Note two important differences between the previous and current systems.
 
Neither Debuff Immunity nor Magic Damage Immunity blocks physical ability damage, reflected damage,Unless the reflected damage is magical damage. or pure damage now, leaving you vulnerable to these sources of damage while you are Magic Immune.
 
Also, since Debuff Resistance and Debuff Immunity are now the same mechanic, abilities that pierce Debuff Immunity now also pierce Debuff Resistance.
Hero Class Updates
The hero class also saw a multitude of changes.
 
There is a new hero class type: Style.
 
These heroes allow the players to build them however they want, depending on their playstyle, by having a fluid primary attribute. The primary attribute is determined by which attribute the hero has the most of. For example, a hero like Pangolier, who gains more agility than strength, can be turned into a strength hero by buying items that give strength.
 
There are also hero class specific perks now, such as damage block for Strength heroes, bonus movement speed for Agility heroes, and cooldown reduction for Intelligence heroes. To find out how these perks work for Universal and Style heroes, keep scrolling.
 
Also forgot to mention that every hero has an innate ability now. Some even more than one.
 
Jungle Economy is now dynamic
Remember when killing neutrals used to give you a lot more gold and experience?
 
Well, that is sort of the case again — for a few minutes, until it gives a lot less again. But wait, it has increased again.
What?
 
Jungle economy is now dynamic — Jungle creep bounties are now 40% higher at the start of the game, but then these bounties decrease by 10% every five minutes for twenty minutes. After that, their bounties increase by 10% every five minutes for twenty minutes, and repeat. You can see the pattern.
There is also a new state, a new damage type, and a new ability type. Even hex has two versions now. There is more, but let's get into the new items because there are a lot. Like a lot.

THE GRAVEYARD

You might have noticed that in the hero changes there was something missing—the talent notes section.
 
Well, that is because talents are no longer part of the game, along with a bunch of other things that were removed as a design choice.
 
The two main problems with the talent system in particular occurred to me while I was in the middle of making the new and revamped talent trees for each hero.
 
It takes every creative quirk that you could give to a hero, as well as being the greatest offender when it comes to power creep.
 
You don't want boring talents that just give +20 damage or -2 second cooldown, but if you make creative talents, then you are either throwing creative ideas away that could be implemented as a base mechanic for the hero or contributing to power creep.
 
Basically, the trade-off here is that players get heroes with more interesting hero tool-kits and less power crept numbers in exchange for the talent system. Should not be too bad of a trade.
 
To summarise, talents are causing power creep, are making hero designs boring, and players in general couldn't care less about them; henceforth, they are removed.
 
 
 
But that is not all.
 
Neutral Items have also been removed.
Neutral Items also suffer from the same problems as talents but with an added spin — they are kind of just pointless.
 
With talents, at least they are interesting but neutral items.. not so much.
 
Even now, with the tokens change, it is still just pointless RNG and power creep wrapped up in one package.
The following structures have also been removed:
 
Outposts
When Outposts were introduced, they used to give tons of experience, which, understandably, was not very enjoyable to play around.
 
A huge part of the charm of DotA is that it gives you creative freedom and allows you to play the game however you want to.
 
With their sole purpose of granting experience stripped, Outposts simply became glorified teleport spots, which is something that is not necessary on the current map; therefore, they are removed.
Tormentors
 
 
 
Tormentors are honestly just not memorable.
 
While they definitely have a decent impact on the game, they just feel redundant and are a chore to take down.
 
They are probably the least necessary structure in the whole game, and as a result, they are removed.
Watchers
 
 
 
 
The services of the small free observer wards, also known as Watchers, are no longer necessary with the map scaled back to a degree.
 
They will be remembered.

Magic Resistance. Works the same way as before, decreases the magic damage dealt to you by a percentage. When this percentage is 100%, it can also be referred to as Magic Immunity.
