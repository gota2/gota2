NEUTRAL ZONES REWORKED
JUNGLE UPDATES
Units attacking non-dominated neutral creeps now lose 250 day-time vision for 4 seconds.
 
Jungle Zones reworked.
 
Heroes and their units now gain 4 bonus health regeneration when traversing through their own native jungle, while they gain 1 bonus mana regeneration and 20 bonus movement speed when traversing through the enemy forests.
 
Neutral Camps were relocated, and new paths have been added to discover while navigating inside the forests.

Neutral creep experience and gold rewards are now dynamic. Works the following way:
 
Neutral creeps now grant 30% bonus gold and experience at the beginning of the game, but then grant 10% less every 5 minutes for 15 minutes.

Offensive and Defensive Power Runes refill three Bottle charges.

CREEP SCALING REWORKED
CREEP UPDATES
Neutral creep scaling reworked.
 
Neutral Creeps now gain a level every 4 minutes. When they level up, they gain 4 bonus attack damage and 0.8 bonus armor. Their levels can be viewed at the same location as hero levels.
 
When a creep is dominated, it instead levels up every time its owner levels up.
 
Mega creeps now spawn with 4 bonus damage and 0.5 bonus armor per consecutive wave.
 
Ancient creeps levels increased:
 
Small ancient creep levels increased from 3 to 8.
Big ancient creep levels increased from 6 to 10.
 
Ancient creeps now have Magic Damage Immunity.
 
Flagbearer Creeps reworked.
 
Flagbearer Creeps now only spawn on the mid-lane and grant 200% of the experience of a melee creep when killed.
 
Inspirational Aura now grants 15% bonus movement speed in a 1200 radius to allied units instead of bonus health regeneration.
 
Lane creeps no longer move faster during night-time.
 
Creep experience penalty when denied increased from 50% to 75%.

NEW HERO CLASS
INNATE ABILITIES
HERO CLASS UPDATES
Talent system has been removed.
 
Heroes now gain class specific benefits depending on their primary attribute:
 
Strength heroes now gain 0.35 damage block per strength.The chance to block is 50% and works on ranged heroes too.
 
 
Agility heroes now gain 0.07% movement speed per agility.
 
Intelligence heroes now gain 0.06% cooldown reduction per intelligence.
 
Heroes now have their own innate abilities.
 
These passive and active abilities differ from being a useful tool early on to being a game-changing late-game ability to abuse. They also differ in leveling: some can be leveled, while others cannot. A few of these innate abilities will be familiar, for example, Invoker's Invoke or Earth Spirit's Stone Remnants; however, others, like Chaotic Luck or Black Grimoire, will probably not be.
 
New Hero Class Type: Style.
 
These heroes allow the players to build them however they want, depending on their playstyle, by having a fluid primary attribute. The primary attribute is determined by which attribute the hero has the most of. For example, a hero like Pangolier, who gains more agility than strength, can be turned into a strength hero by buying items that give strength.
 
Universal Heroes reworked.
 
Hero pool reduced from 34 to 8. These heroes are now Void Spirit, Enigma, Faceless Void, Chaos Knight, Keeper of the Light, IO, Elder Titan, and Weaver.
 
Universal Heroes now fully benefit from every attribute's bonus perks, such as damage block per strength, movement speed per agility, and cooldown reduction per intelligence.
 
Universal Hero damage gain per stat reduced from 0.7 to 0.5.
 
Creep-Heroes reworked.
 
Creep-Heroes are now even more similar to heroes. They are now treated by every ability in the game as heroes, and are capable of leveling up. They level-up when their owner levels up and gain 4 bonus attack damage and 0.8 bonus armor with each level.
 
Heroes no longer move faster during night-time.
 
Heroes no longer get a free Teleport Scroll upon death.
 
Heroes Maximum Level decreased from 30 to 25.
 
Melee Heroes no longer have Innate Damage Block.

NEW   HeroBounty = 2 x [ 2 x (DeadHeroLevel + 0.01 x DeadHeroNetworth)] + StreakGold

Now grants 100% damage reduction to allied structures for 20 seconds. The damage reduction gradually decreases over the duration.

Previously granted bonus armor and health regeneration to allied units in a 900-radius. Now grants 5 bonus armor to allied units that are being attacked for 3 seconds in a 900-radius.

Twin Gates were moved to the location where the Tormentors used to be.
 
Twin Gates do not require to be channeled anymore; the teleporting is instant; however, they place a dispellable debuff on the teleported units that silences and mutes them for 4 seconds. Twin Gates still cannot be used while the user is silenced.
 
Lotus Pools reworked.
 
Lotus Pools were moved to the center of the primary jungles.
 
Healing Lotuses now spawn around the Lotus Pool ready to be picked up every 2 minutes.
 
Lotus Pools now can be channeled to instantly restore 50 health and 100 mana. Once channeled, they dry up and go on cooldown for 3 minutes. While it is on cooldown, the Healing Lotus spawning is delayed and temporarily stopped. If the channeler has a Bottle, then it will be filled with a Water Rune.

STATE AND MECHANIC UPDATES
Spell Damage Reduction has been removed.
 
Spell Damage Amplification reworked into Magic Damage Amplification. Magic Damage Amplification does not increase pure and physical ability damage.
 
Spell Lifesteal reworked into Magic Lifesteal. Magic Lifesteal does not heal from pure and physical ability damage.
 
Magic Lifesteal now has a 400 range buffer. When dealing magic damage outside of this range, it now only heals for 50% of the value.
 
Lifesteal reworked.
 
Lifesteal now has a 400-range buffer. When dealing attack damage outside of this range, it now only heals for 50% of the value.
 
Lifesteal no longer has a creep penalty.
 
Damage Block now has a 400-range buffer. When taking attack damage outside of this range, it no longer mitigates attack damage.
 
Hex reworked. Now has two versions:
 
Breaking Hex. Transforms a unit into a sheep, removing its secondary attributes, for example, magic resistance, evasion, and magic lifesteal.
 
Slowing Hex. Transforms a unit into a frog, decreasing its movement speed to 100. This unit's movement speed cannot be increased while transformed.
 
New State: Paralyze.
 
Paralyzed units are forbidden from casting spells and attacking but can use items. This state is dispelled by any kind of forced movement.
 
Some noteable examples:
 
Paralyzing Cask
Frostbite
Frost Arrows
 
Uphill Miss Chance reworked.
 
The miss chance is now dependent on the level difference between the two units, allowing a maximum of 75% miss chance when there is a three-level difference.
 
Break now can always be dispelled and does not get applied on abilities that are in the ultimate-ability slot.
 
Flying units now elevate from the ground by 190 units, preventing units with a shorter attack range from attacking them and gaining highground advantage miss chance against ranged attackers.
 
Backpack Items are no longer visible to enemy units.
 
Aura effects no longer have a linger duration.
 
Experience Acquisition range decreased from 1500 to 1200
 
 
debuff immunity reworked
DEBUFF IMMUNITY UPDATES
Debuff Immunity has been reworked into Magic Immunity.
 
Magic Immunity consists of two components:
 
Debuff Resistance. Reduces the duration of debuffs by a percentage. When this percentage is 100%, it can also be referred to as Debuff Immunity.
 
Magic Resistance. Works the same way as before. When this percentage is 100%, it can also be referred to as Magic Damage Immunity.
 
Debuff Immunity and Magic Damage Immunity, when combined is what we call Magic Immunity. In this state, while abilities can target you, they won't deal damage and their debuffs immediately end without ever affecting you. The only exception to this is if the ability pierces Magic Immunity.
 
Two noteable differences:
 
Magic Immunity does not block physical damage, reflected damage, or pure damage now, leaving you vulnerable to these sources of damage.
 
Debuff Resistance and Debuff Immunity are now the same mechanic, therefore abilities that pierce Debuff Immunity now also pierce Debuff Resistance.
