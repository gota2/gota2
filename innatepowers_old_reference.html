Hero Class Updates
There is a new hero class type: Style.
 
These heroes allow the players to build them however they want, depending on their playstyle, by having a fluid primary attribute. The primary attribute is determined by which attribute the hero has the most of. For example, a hero like Pangolier, who gains more agility than strength, can be turned into a strength hero by buying items that give strength.
 
Additionally, every hero now has an innate ability.
 
These passive and active abilities differ from being a useful tool early on to being a game-changing late-game ability to abuse. They also differ in leveling: some can be leveled, while others cannot. A few of these innate abilities will be familiar, for example, Invoker's Invoke or Earth Spirit's Stone Remnants; however, others, like Chaotic Luck or Black Grimoire, will probably not be.
 
And more.
 
Rune System Reworked
The rune system has been reworked. Summary:
 
There are now two rune categories: Power Runes and Utility Runes.
 
Power Runes additionally split into two sub-categories: Offensive Power and Defensive Power Runes.
 
Both spawns in the top-side and bot-side of the river every four minutes, starting at minute 4. If an Offensive Power Rune spawns on the top-side, then a Defensive Power Rune will spawn on the bot-side.
 
Utility Runes spawn in the main jungle of both sides every 2 minutes, starting at minute 2. The first Utility Rune will always be a Water Rune, and every third will always be a Bounty Rune.
 
To find out more about the Rune system and the list of Runes, keep scrolling.
Debuff Immunity reworked
Debuff Immunity has been reworked into Magic Immunity to be more understandable and systematic, both function-wise and naming-wise.
 
Magic Immunity is made up of two components:
 
Magic Resistance. Works the same way as before, decreases the magic damage dealt to you by a percentage. When this percentage is 100%, we can also refer to it as Magic Damage Immunity.
 
Debuff Resistance. This one simply reduces the duration of debuffs by a percentage. When this percentage is 100%, we can also refer to it as Debuff Immunity.
 
Debuff Immunity and Magic Damage Immunity, when combined is what we call Magic Immunity. In this state, while abilities can target you, they won't deal damage and their debuffs immediately end without ever affecting you. Well, unless they pierce Magic Immunity.
 
Note two important differences between the previous and current systems.
 
Neither Debuff Immunity nor Magic Damage Immunity blocks physical ability damage, reflected damage, or pure damage now, leaving you vulnerable to these sources of damage while you are Magic Immune.
 
Also, since Debuff Resistance and Debuff Immunity are now the same mechanic, abilities that pierce Debuff Immunity now also pierce Debuff Resistance.
Economy Updates
Events that include enemy heroes and structures now provide reliable gold, while your base gold per minute and other passive sources of gold now provide unreliable gold to make risk-taking more rewarding. Additionally, neutral creep economy is now dynamic, meaning that the gold and experience bounty of neutral creeps change constantly throughout the game.
Hero Kill Formula Reworked
When you are killed, it is only your own net worth and current level that matter now. Kills no longer have a base bounty or a team networth factor; also, the hero kill assist formula is directly tied to the hero kill formula now, making the received bounties more consistent and logical.
Roshan Updates
Killing Roshan is harder than ever, but also ever so rewarding. When Roshan is killed, the next 3 creepwaves that spawn will consist of mega creeps and a catapult, making the highground siege with the Aegis of the Immortal a lot easier than before. Also, the rewards for killing Roshan are a bit different as well.

THE GRAVEYARD

You might have noticed that in the hero changes there was something missing - the talent notes section.
 
Well, that is because talents are no longer part of the game, along with a bunch of other things that were removed as a design choice.
 
The two main problems with the talent system in particular are that it takes every creative quirk that you could give to a hero, as well as being the greatest offender when it comes to power creep.
 
You don't want boring talents that just give +20 damage or -2 second cooldown, but if you make creative talents, then you are either throwing away creative ideas that could be implemented as a base mechanic for the hero or contributing to power creep.
 
Basically, the trade-off here is that players get heroes with more interesting hero toolkits and less power crept numbers in exchange for the talent system. It should not be too bad of a trade.
But that is not all.
 
Neutral Items have also been removed.
Neutral Items also suffer from the same problems as talents but with an added spin - they are kind of just pointless.
 
With talents, at least they are interesting but neutral items.. not so much.
 
Even now, with the tokens change, it is still just pointless RNG and power creep wrapped up in one package.
The following structures have also been removed:
 
Outposts
 
 
When Outposts were introduced, they used to give tons of experience, which, understandably, was not very enjoyable to play around.
 
A huge part of the charm of DotA is that it gives you creative freedom and allows you to play the game however you want to.
 
With their sole purpose of granting experience stripped, Outposts simply became glorified teleport spots, which is something that is not necessary on the current map; therefore, they are removed.
Tormentors
 
 
 
Tormentors are honestly just not memorable.
 
While they definitely have a decent impact on the game, they just feel redundant and are a chore to take down.
 
They are probably the least necessary structure in the whole game, and as a result, they are removed.
Watchers
 
 
 
 
The services of the small free observer wards, also known as Watchers, are no longer necessary with the map scaled back to a degree.
 
They will be remembered.
And that is every major removal.

DEBUFF IMMUNITY UPDATES
Debuff Immunity has been reworked into Magic Immunity.
 
Magic Immunity consists of two components:
 
Debuff Resistance. Reduces the duration of debuffs by a percentage. When this percentage is 100%, it can also be referred to as Debuff Immunity.
 
Magic Resistance. Works the same way as before, decreases the magic damage dealt to you by a percentage. When this percentage is 100%, it can also be referred to as Magic Immunity.
 
Debuff Immunity and Magic Damage Immunity, when combined is what we call Magic Immunity. In this state, while abilities can target you, they won't deal damage and their debuffs immediately end without ever affecting you. The only exception to this is if the ability pierces Magic Immunity.
 
Two noteable differences:
 
Neither Debuff Immunity nor Magic Immunity blocks physical damage, reflected damage, or pure damage now, leaving you vulnerable to these sources of damage.
 
Debuff Resistance and Debuff Immunity are now the same mechanic, therefore abilities that pierce Debuff Immunity now also pierce Debuff Resistance.
